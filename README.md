# Tag You’re It

**Tag You’re It** is a campus help network that lets students get instant, verified help from nearby peers for academics, tech, interviews, and more. Instead of forcing students to search for tutors and book sessions later, help comes to them in real time.

## Why it matters

On most campuses, students do not fail because help does not exist. They struggle because asking for help is awkward, slow, and too dependent on confidence, timing, and organization.

Traditional tutoring platforms often make students:
- search for support,
- compare profiles,
- message strangers,
- and schedule help for later.

That creates friction right when students are already overwhelmed.

## What Tag You’re It does

When a student is stuck, they send a **tag**.

Nearby verified peers get a short window to catch it.  
If no one catches it, the tag becomes visible on a campus map until someone responds.

Students can request help with:
- Calculus
- Excel
- Python
- Resume reviews
- Interview prep
- Presentation practice
- And more

Every helpful session builds a **campus skill graph** that shows who consistently helps with what, under real pressure.

## Core features

- Real-time help requests
- Nearby peer matching
- Campus map visibility
- Skill-based profiles
- Verified university email access
- XP / points system for helping others
- Leaderboard for contributors
- SOS support flow for urgent situations

## Why this is different

This is not just a tutoring app.

It is a system for:
- reducing help-seeking friction,
- making peer support immediate,
- and turning real helping behavior into a visible record of skill and leadership.

A GPA shows that a student passed a class.  
A history of repeated peer help shows that a student can explain, adapt, and perform under pressure.

## How it works

1. A student opens the app and submits a tag.
2. Nearby verified peers get notified.
3. A peer catches the tag and starts helping.
4. The session is tracked and rewarded with XP.
5. Over time, the app builds a skill-based reputation graph.

## Impact

Tag You’re It helps students:
- get faster help,
- feel less alone,
- and build confidence asking questions.

It also gives campuses a better way to understand where students are struggling and which peers are consistently stepping up.
